## Design Context

### Users
Recruiters screening candidates, hiring managers, and engineers — arriving from LinkedIn or GitHub where they see a link. Two modes of consumption: quick scanners (recruiters/HMs skimming for signal) and deeper readers (engineers evaluating project quality). The site must reward both modes without compromising for either.

### Brand Personality
**Driven. Systematic. Thorough.**

Not flashy. Not humble. William is someone who sweats the details quietly and delivers — the interface should feel the same way. Purposeful, not performative. Every element earns its place.

Emotional goal: confidence and quiet authority. Visitors should leave thinking "this person knows what they're doing" — not "wow cool website."

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark primary. The audience (engineers, technical recruiters) sits at a screen in a focused context.
- **Reading color**: Light blue `oklch(0.8877 0.0288 218.97)` — dominant body text color; makes content readable against dark bg
- **Brand colors**: Soft purple `oklch(0.5579 0.0909 318.07)` for structural elements and labels; warm amber `oklch(0.6823 0.176 46.72)` for accents and emphasis
- **Visual richness**: Denser than Emil Kowalski, not as sparse as Lee Rob or Paco. Content should feel substantial.
- **Interactivity**: Present and purposeful — like Brittany Chiang's site. Not scroll-jacking, not gimmicky.
- **NOT**: Blocky card grids, résumé-dump work history, scroll hijacking, glassmorphism, generic shadows

**References**:
- Brittany Chiang (visual interest + interactivity level) — but more curated, less chronological
- Emil Kowalski (content quality and presentation) — but more visual substance

**Anti-references**: leerob.com (too bare), liveblocks.io/blog (too blocky), rauno.me (scroll hijacking)

### Hero Direction
Static. No rotating text — removed permanently. The current statement ("Technical Lead at InfoTrack" + positioning line) is the final direction. Any future hero work should build on this static foundation, not reintroduce animation for its own sake.

### Multi-Page Architecture
The site will expand with additional pages (exact pages TBD). Design decisions should account for this: navigation patterns, page transitions, and layout shells should be built for a multi-page site, not a single-page scroll.

### Design Principles
1. **Curated over comprehensive** — show fewer things with more depth. Quality of presentation > quantity of content.
2. **Earned interactivity** — every animation and hover state conveys information or confirms action. Nothing decorative-only.
3. **Hierarchy through contrast** — use size, weight, and color to guide the eye. Purple for structure and labels, amber to draw attention, blue to be read.
4. **Code as content** — syntax-highlighted code blocks are a first-class element of the design, not an afterthought.
5. **Dark and deliberate** — the dark theme isn't a style choice, it's the right environment for the audience and the content.

### Accessibility
- **Target**: WCAG AAA
- **Reduced motion**: Always respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Animations must have non-animated fallbacks. No motion that cannot be disabled.
- **Color contrast**: AAA minimum (7:1 for normal text, 4.5:1 for large text). Verify all foreground/background combinations.
- **Typography**: Sufficient size, line-height, and letter-spacing for readability at all breakpoints.
